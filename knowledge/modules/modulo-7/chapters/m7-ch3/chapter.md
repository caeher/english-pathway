## Subject-Verb Inversion & Emphatic Structures

### Investment with negative expressions
When we start with a negative expression, the subject and auxiliary are **inverted**:

| Normal | Inverted (emphatic) |
|--------|---------------------|
| I have **never** seen such beauty. | **Never have** I seen such beauty. |
| She **not only** sings but also dances. | **Not only does** she sing, **but** she also dances. |
| He **hardly** arrived **when** it started raining. | **Hardly had** he arrived **when** it started raining. |

### Expressions that cause inversion
| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| **Never** | **Never have** I been so happy. |
| **Rarely** | **Rarely do** we see such talent. |
| **Seldom** | **Seldom does** she complain. |
| **Not onlyΟΔβ²§¬Π but also** | **Not only does** he cook, **but** he also cleans. |
| **HardlyΟΔβ²§¬Π when** | **Hardly had** I sat down **when** the phone rang. |
| **No soonerΟΔβ²§¬Π than** | **No sooner had** I left **than** it started raining. |
| **Only after** | **Only after** finishing **did** I rest. |
| **Little** | **Little did** I know it would rain. |

### Cleft sentences ΟΔβ²§βÄù Oraciones hendidas
To give **emphasis** to a part of speech:

| Normal | Cleft (emphasis) |
|--------|------------------|
| John broke the window. | **It was John who** broke the window. |
| I need more time. | **What I need is** more time. |
| She loves music. | **What she loves is** music. |
| The price surprised me. | **It was the price that** surprised me. |
