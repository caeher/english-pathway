## Reported Speech — Estilo Indirecto (Statements)

### ¿Qué es?
- **Direct:** "I **am** happy," she said.
- **Reported:** She said (that) she **was** happy.
- Reportamos lo que alguien dijo SIN usar comillas.

### Said vs. Told
| Verb | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **said** | without person (said that...) | She **said** she was happy. |
| **told** | with person (told someone that...) | She **told** me she was happy. |
- ❌ ~~She said me...~~ → ✅ She **told** me...
- ❌ ~~She told that...~~ → ✅ She **said** that...

### Backshift — Cambio de tiempos
| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| "I **am** happy." | She said she **was** happy. |
| "I **like** chocolate." | He said he **liked** chocolate. |
| "I **am working**." | She said she **was working**. |
| "I **have finished**." | He said he **had finished**. |
| "I **went** to Paris." | She said she **had gone** to Paris. |
| "I **will** help you." | He said he **would** help me. |
| "I **can** swim." | She said she **could** swim. |

### Pronoun & Time changes
| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| I / me | he / she / him / her |
| my | his / her |
| we / us | they / them |
| today | that day |
| yesterday | the day before |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| here | there |
| now | then |
| this | that |

### "That" is optional
- She said **(that)** she was happy.
- He told me **(that)** he liked chocolate.
