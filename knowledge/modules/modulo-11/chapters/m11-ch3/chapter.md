## Modal Verbs — Expresando matices en inglés

Los verbos modales añaden significado extra al verbo principal: habilidad, posibilidad, obligación, permiso, consejo…

### Modales por función

| Función | Modal | Ejemplo |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **Habilidad** | can, could | *I can swim. / I could swim when I was 5.* |
| **Posibilidad** | may, might, could | *It might rain tomorrow.* |
| **Permiso** | can, may, could | *May I use your phone?* |
| **Obligación** | must, have to | *You must wear a seatbelt.* |
| **Prohibición** | mustn't, can't | *You mustn't smoke here.* |
| **Consejo** | should, ought to | *You should see a doctor.* |
| **No necesario** | don't have to | *You don't have to come.* |

### ⚠️ Diferencias sutiles

| Contraste | Explicación |
|-----------|-------------|
| **must** vs **have to** | must = obligación personal; have to = obligación externa |
| **mustn't** vs **don't have to** | mustn't = prohibido; don't have to = no es necesario |
| **can** vs **may** | can = informal; may = formal (para permiso) |
| **should** vs **must** | should = consejo; must = obligación fuerte |

### Escala de fuerza
`must > should > could > might` (de más fuerte a más débil)

> 💡 **Recuerda:** Los modales NO cambian con la persona (*he **can*** ✓, ~~he cans~~ ✗) y van seguidos del verbo en forma **base** (*must **go*** ✓, ~~must goes~~ ✗).
