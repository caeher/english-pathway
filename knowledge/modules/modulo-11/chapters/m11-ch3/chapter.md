## Modal Verbs — Expressing nuances in English

Modal verbs add extra meaning to the main verb: ability, possibility, obligation, permission, advice...

### Modals by function

| Function | Modal | Ejemplo |
|---------|-------|---------|
| **Habilidad** | can, could | *I can swim. / I could swim when I was 5.* |
| **Posibilidad** | may, might, could | *It might rain tomorrow.* |
| **Permiso** | can, may, could | *May I use your phone?* |
| **Obligation** | must, have to | *You must wear a seatbelt.* |
| **Prohibition** | mustn't, can't | *You mustn't smoke here.* |
| **Consejo** | should, ought to | *You should see a doctor.* |
| **No necesario** | don't have to | *You don't have to come.* |

### ⚠️ Diferencias sutiles

| Contraste | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **must** vs **have to** | must = personal obligation; have to = external obligation |
| **mustn't** vs **don't have to** | mustn't = forbidden; don't have to = it is not necessary |
| **can** vs **may** | can = informal; may = formal (for permission) |
| **should** vs **must** | should = advice; must = strong obligation |

### Strength scale
`must > should > could > might` (from strongest to weakest)

> 💡 **Remember:** Modals DO NOT change with the person (*he **can*** ✓, ~~he cans~~ ✗) and are followed by the verb in **base** form (*must **go*** ✓, ~~must goes~~ ✗).
