## Want to, Like to, Need to

### Want to (querer)
- I **want to** travel. / She **wants to** learn English.
- **Neg:** I **don't want to** go. / He **doesn't want to** eat.
- **Q:** **Do** you **want to** play? / **Does** she **want to** come?

### Like + verb-ing / Like + to + verb (gustar)
- I **like swimming**. = I **like to swim**. (ambas correctas)
- She **likes reading**. = She **likes to read**.
- **Neg:** I **don't like cooking**. / He **doesn't like running**.
- **Q:** **Do** you **like dancing**? / **Does** she **like studying**?

### Need to (necesitar)
- I **need to** study. / She **needs to** work.
- **Neg:** You **don't need to** hurry. / He **doesn't need to** come.
- **Q:** **Do** you **need to** leave? / **Does** she **need to** go?

### Would like (more polite than want)
- I**'d like** a coffee. = I **would like** a coffee.
- I**'d like to** travel. = I **would like to** travel.
- **Q:** **Would** you **like** some tea?

### Summary
| Verb | Example | Translation |
|------|---------|-------------|
| want to | I want to go. | Quiero ir. |
| like + ing | I like reading. | Me gusta leer. |
| need to | I need to study. | Necesito estudiar. |
| would like | I'd like a coffee. | I would like a coffee. |
