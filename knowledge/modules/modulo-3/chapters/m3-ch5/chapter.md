## Can & Could — Habilidades y Posibilidades

### Can — Presente
| Use | Example |
|-----|---------|
| **Ability** (habilidad) | I **can** swim. / She **can** speak French. |
| **Permission** (permiso) | You **can** sit here. / **Can** I use your phone? |
| **Possibility** | We **can** go tomorrow. |

### Negative: cannot / can't
- I **can't** swim. / She **can't** drive.

### Questions: Can + subject + verb?
- **Can** you swim? — Yes, I can. / No, I can't.

### Could — Pasado + Cortesía
| Use | Example |
|-----|---------|
| **Past ability** | I **could** swim when I was 5. |
| **Polite request** | **Could** you help me, please? |
| **Possibility (uncertain)** | It **could** rain later. |

### Negative: couldn't
- I **couldn't** swim when I was 3.

### Can vs. Could
| | Present | Past | Polite |
|--|---------|------|--------|
| ✅ | I **can** swim. | I **could** swim at 5. | **Could** you help? |
| ❌ | I **can't** drive. | I **couldn't** read at 3. | — |

### Be able to (alternativa)
- **Can** no tiene futuro ni infinitivo. Usa **be able to**:
  - Futuro: I **will be able to** swim next year.
  - Infinitivo: I want **to be able to** speak French.
- **Could** = habilidad general en el pasado. **Was/were able to** = logro puntual:
  - I **could** swim when I was 5. (habilidad)
  - I **was able to** fix the car yesterday. (logro concreto)

> 💡 "Can" NO lleva "to": I can swim (NOT: ~~I can to swim~~)
