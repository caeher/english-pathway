## Advanced Role-Play — Multi-scene simulations with unexpected twists

They are no longer simple dialogues. Here you will face **realistic simulations** with multiple actors, context changes and decisions that have consequences.

### The 5 Dimensions of Advanced Simulation

| Dimension | What does it imply? |
|---|---|
| 🏢 **Contexto complejo** | Situations with multiple factors and stakeholders |
| 🔄 **Giros inesperados** | Changes that force linguistic adaptation in real time |
| 🎭 **Registry change** | Go from formal to informal depending on who is speaking to you |
| ⚖️ **Conflict resolution** | Mediation, negotiation and persuasion |
| 🧠 **Decision making** | Evaluate options and communicate informed decisions |

### Simulation Scenarios

| Escenario | Main challenge |
|---|---|
| 🏢 **Crisis in the company** | Comunicar malas noticias y proponer soluciones |
| 🏥 **In the emergency room** | Describe complex symptoms and follow medical instructions |
| ✈️ **Viaje cancelado** | Negotiate with airline, hotel and insurance |
| 🎓 **Project defense** | Present and defend a proposal before a panel |
| 🤝 **Neighborhood mediation** | Resolve conflict between parties with opposing interests |

### Change of Registry — The Linguistic Chameleon

The SAME idea is expressed differently depending on the context:

| Registro | Ejemplo |
|---|---|
| 🤙 **Casual** | "Hey, can you help me out with this?" |
| 🤝 **Informal** | "We need to talk about this ASAP." |
| 👔 **Formal** | "I'd like to schedule a meeting to discuss this matter." |
| 🎩 **Ultra-formal** | "Could we perhaps revisit this at a more convenient time?" |
| 📧 **Formal escrito** | "I would be grateful if you could assist with this request." |

### From Direct to Diplomatic

| Directo ❌ | Diplomat ✅ |
|---|---|
| "You're wrong." | "I see your point, but I'd like to offer a different perspective." |
| "That's a bad idea." | "That's an interesting approach. Have you considered...?" |
| "We can't do that." | "That might be challenging, but let me see what we can do." |
| "Your work isn't good enough." | "I appreciate your effort. There are a few areas we could improve." |
| "No." | "I'm afraid that won't be possible at this time." |

> 💡 **Tip:** In pressure situations, breathe and use a transition phrase: "That's a good point. Let me think about that." This gives you time to formulate a diplomatic response.
