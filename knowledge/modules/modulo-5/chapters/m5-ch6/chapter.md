## Passive Voice — Advanced Practice

### Repaso: Activa → Pasiva
1. The **object** of the active → **subject** of the passive.
2. Conjugate **BE** in the same tense as the active verb.
3. Add the **past participle**.
4. (Optional) Add **by + agent**.

### Passive with ditransitive verbs (two objects)
- Active: They **gave** *me* *a present*.
- Passive 1: **I** was given a present. (most common)
- Passive 2: **A present** was given to me.

### Passive with modal verbs
| Active | Passive |
|--------|---------|
| You **must do** the homework. | The homework **must be done**. |
| They **can fix** the problem. | The problem **can be fixed**. |
| We **should clean** the room. | The room **should be cleaned**. |
| They **might cancel** the event. | The event **might be cancelled**. |

### When NOT to use the passive
- ❌ Intransitive verbs (without object): happen, arrive, die, sleep
  - ~~The accident was happened.~~ → The accident happened.
- ❌ Cuando suena raro o innecesario:
  - ~~A good time was had by us.~~ → We had a good time.

### Pasiva impersonal (It is said that...)
| Active | Passive |
|--------|---------|
| People **say** he is rich. | **It is said** that he is rich. / He **is said** to be rich. |
| They **believe** she is innocent. | **It is believed** that she is innocent. |
| Everyone **knows** the answer. | **It is known** that... / The answer **is known**. |

### Common collocations in passive
- be **born**: I **was born** in 1990.
- be **made** of/from: This table **is made of** wood.
- be **known** for: She **is known for** her kindness.
- be **interested** in: I **am interested in** art.
- be **surprised** at/by: We **were surprised by** the news.
