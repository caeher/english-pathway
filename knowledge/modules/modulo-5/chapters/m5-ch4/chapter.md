## Participles â€” The Participles

### âš ï¸ Important: These are not verb tenses
Many people believe that "present participle" and "past participle" are tenses (like present or past). **They are not.** They are **forms of the verb** that are used in different structures.

| Forma | How it is formed | Ejemplo |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Present participle** | base + **-ing** | working, eating, running |
| **Past participle** | -ed (regulares) o forma irregular | worked, eaten, run |

### Present participle (-ing)
- **Uso 1:** Tiempos continuos: I **am working**. / She **was eating**.
- **Use 2:** After verbs of perception: I **saw** her **running**.
- **Use 3:** As an adjective: a **boring** movie, an **interesting** book.
- **Use 4:** Gerund (as a noun): **Swimming** is fun.

### Past participle (-ed / irregular)
- **Uso 1:** Tiempos perfectos: I **have eaten**. / She **had finished**.
- **Uso 2:** Voz pasiva: The car **was stolen**. / The house **is being built**.
- **Use 3:** As an adjective: a **broken** window, **written** instructions.
- **Use 4:** After "get": I **got tired**.

### Rules of the past participle
| Tipo | Training | Ejemplos |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Regulares** | base + -ed | worked, played, studied |
| **Irregulares** | forma especial | eaten, gone, written, seen |
| **-ed spelling** | same as Past Simple | walked, wanted, stopped |
