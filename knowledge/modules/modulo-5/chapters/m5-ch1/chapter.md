## Present Perfect — Presente Perfecto

### Formación: have/has + past participle (3ª columna)
| Subject | Auxiliary | Past Participle | Example |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| I / You / We / They | **have ('ve)** | visited | I'**ve visited** Paris. |
| He / She / It | **has ('s)** | seen | She'**s seen** that movie. |

### Past Participles
- **Regular:** work → work**ed**, play → play**ed**, visit → visit**ed**
- **Irregular:** go → **gone**, see → **seen**, eat → **eaten**, be → **been**, do → **done**, have → **had**, write → **written**, speak → **spoken**, take → **taken**, buy → **bought**

### Key Words
| Word | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **ever** | questions | Have you **ever** been to Japan? |
| **never** | negative experience | I have **never** eaten sushi. |
| **already** | completed (sooner than expected) | I've **already** finished. |
| **yet** | neg/questions (still pending) | I haven't finished **yet**. / Have you finished **yet**? |
| **just** | very recently | I've **just** arrived. |

### Negative & Questions
- **Neg:** I **haven't** visited Rome. / She **hasn't** seen it.
- **Q:** **Have** you visited Rome? / **Has** she seen it?
- **Short:** Yes, I have. / No, she hasn't.

> 💡 Present Perfect = experiencia SIN decir cuándo. Si dices cuándo → Past Simple.
> ✅ I've visited Paris. (alguna vez) / ❌ ~~I've visited Paris last year.~~ → I visited Paris last year.
